About Me

Welcome! I'm Jake Schwartz, a graduate student at UNC Chapel Hill pursuing my Master's in Information Science with a focus on environmental data analytics and renewable energy systems.

I combine technical expertise in GIS, Python, and data analysis with hands-on experience in renewable energy siting, environmental modeling, and geospatial data operations. Currently working as a Data Operations Intern at Paces, I specialize in managing large-scale environmental datasets to support critical mapping initiatives.

My background includes utility-scale solar project development at Sonder Energy, greenhouse gas analysis at Environmental Defense Fund, and research on sustainable energy systems and knowledge graphs.

Career Interests & Experience

Renewable Energy Development

Passionate about advancing clean energy solutions through data-driven site selection, environmental analysis, and project development. Experienced with utility-scale solar projects (10-200 MW) and small modular reactor research for green hydrogen production.

Environmental Data Science

Combining GIS analysis, machine learning, and data operations to support environmental decision-making. Current work involves managing large geospatial datasets for hazard mapping and environmental monitoring for state and federal use.

Climate & Sustainability Analytics

Applying quantitative methods to address climate challenges including greenhouse gas accounting, life cycle assessment, and energy systems modeling. Interested in leveraging data analytics to accelerate the clean energy transition.
